## Inventory (by shot list category)

### 01-artwork — Fly paintings (15 distinct pieces captured)

This is an absolute treasure. Every piece is more sophisticated than I anticipated. The artist has a real point of view: heavily-rendered fly figures (with anatomical accuracy — visible wings, hairs, joint structure) placed in casually-sketched human contexts. The contrast is the joke. The fly is taken seriously; the human world isn't.

Pieces inventoried:

1. **Fly with paintbrush sketching nature** — minimalist, fly-as-artist
2. **Fly fallen asleep in recliner watching TV** with Z's — domestic exhaustion
3. **Fly art class** — three flies at easels painting a fourth fly on a pedestal (recursive: flies making art about flies)
4. **Cowboy fly riding a horse** — references the menu item "Cowboy Curtis"
5. **Two flies playing hopscotch**
6. **Fly fly-fishing** — using a fly as bait to catch a fly. *The pun is the piece.*
7. **Fly buskers** — tuba and trumpet players, audience flies, tip jar
8. **Two flies on a candlelit dinner date** — wine bottle, curtains, real romance
9. **Suburban fly couple** — one mowing the lawn, one lounging under a patio umbrella with a cocktail
10. **Two flies in a lightsaber duel** — blue saber vs. red saber
11. **Two flies kissing** with a cloud of hearts above
12. **Fly traversing a desert** with footprint trail and sun beating down
13. **Fly eye exam** — chart starts "FLY PROL IZHN BVRS..." patient says "K"
14. **Fly on a toilet on the phone**
15. **Eight flies queued for the men's/women's restrooms**
16. **Two flies (different sizes) on a footprint trail** — parent-and-child quiet moment
17. **Drunk fly approaching a fly on a scale** — diet-vs-drinking
18. **Fly parade buskers** — tuba and trumpet musicians performing

That's ~17 distinct pieces visible across the 15 P0 artwork shots. **Every single one is named, conceptually-complete, and ownership-photographable.** This is the website's content moat.

**The artist has a consistent visual language across all pieces:**
- Real photographed fly bodies (or hyper-realistic painted flies) with hand-sketched limbs and props
- Pencil/graphite line work for environmental context
- Occasional color accents (Star Wars sabers, hearts, the green fly in the art class)
- Thin black frames
- Off-white paper

This style is replicable for site spot-illustrations IF we commission. If we don't commission, the existing paintings — properly photographed without glass reflections — are themselves the illustration system.

### 04-details — Walls and color zoning

A major brand discovery: **the room is not monochromatic.** It's color-zoned:

- **Bar walls: gray-lavender** (`#797A7E`) — calming, lets the marble bar and back-bar art pop
- **Restroom corridor walls: yellow-mustard** (`#B9A651`) — playful, energetic
- **Dining room walls: presumably the fire-engine red** (we didn't get a clean shot — return-visit task)

This is a sophisticated environmental design move. The website's color system should reflect zoning — different brand color "rooms" on different pages or sections.

### 04-details — Restroom sign (single piece, world-class)

The men's/women's bathroom signs are the brand thesis in one object: a toilet rendered on a black-and-white checkerboard background, with a stylized fly at the flush handle position. Mounted on a yellow wall. **This is the visual lockup of every brand element working together** — checkerboard + fly motif + yellow accent zone. The website should reference this object directly somewhere (a 404 page? the restroom-line painting on the FAQ page?).

## One observation on photo handling

A couple shots include identifiable people (the staff member at the POS, a customer at the counter). Standard practice for a public-facing restaurant site:

- **Crop them out** of any image we publish, OR
- **Get explicit verbal/written consent** if Kara approves the shot specifically and the staff member agrees, OR
- **Blur the face** (less ideal — looks evasive)

Easiest path: when we use the back-bar shots, crop to the bar/wall content and exclude people, OR get permission as part of the engagement once Kara has agreed to the project. Either way, we don't ship those photos with identifiable faces in Phase 1 of the spec build.
